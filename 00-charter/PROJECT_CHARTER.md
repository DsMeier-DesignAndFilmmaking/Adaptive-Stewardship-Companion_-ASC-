# Adaptive Stewardship Companion (ASC)
## Project Charter & Operating Instructions

This is the source-of-truth charter for the ASC repository. All strategy, research, design, engineering, and business planning in this repo should trace back to the principles below. See `../ROADMAP.md` for the current sequence of active work, and `charter-review-2026-07-04.md` in this folder for an initial critique and open risks to validate.

## Repository Purpose

This repository is the primary product workspace for Adaptive Stewardship Companion (ASC). It is independent from any portfolio or case study. While portfolio materials may reference this project, this repository should be treated as the source of truth for product strategy, research, architecture, design, engineering, and business planning.

Every artifact created here should contribute toward building a production-ready software platform capable of supporting real customers and commercial pilots.

## Project Identity

**Working Product Name:** Adaptive Stewardship Companion (ASC)

The product name may evolve through future branding and positioning research.

Act as a multidisciplinary product team composed of: Product Strategist, Founder Advisor, Systems Designer, Technical Product Manager, AI Product Designer, UX/UI Architect, Information Architect, Service Designer, Environmental Systems Researcher, Conservation Technology Researcher, Outdoor Recreation Industry Analyst, Landscape Systems Thinker, Business Strategist, Startup Advisor.

- **Evidence-Informed Insight** — supported by analogous industries or established practices but not yet validated in the target market.

## Mission

Design and bring to market a production-ready software platform that helps outdoor organizations make better operational decisions while improving both visitor experiences and ecological outcomes.

The platform should intelligently balance: visitor experience, ecological health, landscape stewardship, conservation goals, operational efficiency, staff decision-making.

ASC is not another outdoor recreation application. ASC is an adaptive intelligence layer that helps organizations coordinate people and landscapes together.

## Long-Term Vision

Become the leading adaptive operational intelligence platform for organizations managing outdoor experiences and living landscapes.

Potential customers include: guest ranches, outdoor resorts, eco-lodges, state parks, national parks, conservation organizations, land trusts, botanical gardens, nature centers, outdoor education organizations, wildlife preserves, adventure tourism operators, regenerative agriculture properties, working ranches open to visitors.

## Product Thesis

Outdoor organizations already collect large amounts of information. They lack adaptive intelligence that continuously converts changing environmental conditions, operational realities, visitor behavior, and ecological signals into better daily decisions. Adaptive Stewardship Companion becomes that adaptive operational intelligence layer.

## North Star

Adaptive Stewardship Companion exists to help outdoor organizations make better operational decisions that simultaneously improve: visitor experiences, ecological resilience, operational effectiveness.

Success is measured by improvements in real-world outcomes — not software usage alone.

## Core Product Philosophy

Most existing outdoor software helps people find places. ASC helps organizations guide people through places in ways that improve both visitor outcomes and ecological outcomes.

The landscape should always be treated as an active participant in the system rather than passive scenery. Every recommendation should consider both human needs and landscape needs.

## Core Design Principles

**Human-Centered:** Reduce uncertainty. Increase confidence. Reduce cognitive load. Provide context-aware guidance. Increase trust in recommendations.

**Landscape-Centered:** Protect ecological integrity. Support restoration. Reduce environmental impact. Respect seasonal dynamics. Respond to changing environmental conditions. Treat ecological health as a first-class design input.

**Systems-Centered:** Never optimize one component at the expense of the whole system. Every recommendation should consider interactions among: people, land, weather, ecology, infrastructure, staff, operations, time, risk, resources.

**Operationally Valuable:** Every feature must improve operational decision-making. The platform should improve how organizations make decisions — not simply display information.

## Product Boundaries

ASC is NOT intended to become: reservation software, Property Management System (PMS), GIS platform, trail mapping application, asset management software, CRM, CMS, accounting software, marketing platform.

Instead, ASC should integrate with existing systems where appropriate while providing adaptive intelligence above them.

## Competitive Position

ASC is not competing to become the best reservation platform or mapping platform. ASC competes by becoming the adaptive operational intelligence layer that connects existing outdoor technologies into a coordinated decision system.

## Primary Users

**Primary operational users:** General Managers, Ranch Managers, Stewardship Directors, Conservation Managers, Guest Experience Managers, Naturalists, Trail Managers, Outdoor Educators, Operations Coordinators, Seasonal Staff Supervisors.

**Secondary users:** Visitors, Volunteers, Seasonal Staff, Researchers.

Organizations purchase software. People adopt software. Design for the people who make daily operational decisions.

## Product Goals

The platform should help organizations answer questions such as: Which experience is most appropriate today? Which areas require ecological recovery? Where should visitors be encouraged? Where should visitation be reduced? Which guests need additional guidance? How should recommendations change as conditions change? Which operational bottlenecks are emerging? Which actions produce measurable stewardship improvements? How can visitor satisfaction and ecological resilience improve together?

## Product Categories

Research should focus on opportunities at the intersection of: environmental systems, landscape stewardship, conservation, outdoor recreation, hospitality, experiential design, adaptive management, ecological monitoring, AI-assisted decision support, visitor experience, landscape operations.

## Product Principles

Every recommendation should follow these principles: AI recommends, humans remain accountable. Every recommendation explains why it exists. Confidence accompanies every prediction. Ecological considerations are first-class inputs. Recommendations degrade gracefully when data quality is low. Simplicity is preferred over unnecessary complexity. Existing operational workflows should be augmented rather than replaced. Every feature should improve a measurable operational outcome.

## Decision Framework

Prioritize ideas using the following order:

1. Real customer pain
2. Ecological impact
3. Customer willingness to pay
4. Operational value
5. Product differentiation
6. Technical feasibility
7. AI leverage
8. Long-term scalability

Avoid features that are technically interesting but solve insignificant problems.

## Validation Standard

No feature should advance into MVP planning without evidence for: a recurring customer problem, measurable operational value, workflow improvement, customer willingness to change, customer willingness to pay.

Favor customer interviews, field observations, pilot programs, and operational evidence over assumptions.

## Product Development Workflow

Every initiative should move through this lifecycle: Industry Research → Market Landscape Analysis → Customer Discovery → Jobs-To-Be-Done Research → Problem Definition → Opportunity Assessment → Product Strategy → Service Design → Systems Architecture → Information Architecture → UX Architecture → Product Design → AI Decision Design → Prototype Development → MVP Definition → Pilot Planning → Production Roadmap → Product Launch → Continuous Learning.

Do not skip stages unless prior evidence demonstrates completion. (See ROADMAP.md for how this repo sequences and time-boxes these stages in practice.)

## Product Operating Model

Research → Insights → Problem Statements → Opportunity Areas → Concept Concepts → Validation → Requirements → Architecture → Design → Prototype → Pilot → Product.

This operating model should guide planning, documentation, and engineering.

## Success Metrics

**Ecological:** Habitat recovery, vegetation health, wildlife disturbance reduction, trail degradation, restoration progress, ecological resilience.

**Visitor:** Confidence, satisfaction, participation, repeat visitation, reduced uncertainty, reduced perceived crowding.

**Operational:** Staff efficiency, decision speed, maintenance prioritization, incident reduction, resource allocation, operational visibility.

**Business:** Pilot customers, customer retention, Net Revenue Retention (NRR), Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR), Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), expansion revenue.

## Working Style

Approach this repository as though you are an early employee at a venture-backed startup. Challenge assumptions respectfully. Identify risks early. Prefer evidence over opinion. Recommend iterative validation instead of speculative planning. Think in systems rather than features. Prioritize customer value over technical novelty.

Whenever uncertainty exists, clearly state: what is known, what is assumed, what requires validation.

Do not optimize for portfolio presentation. Optimize for building a commercially viable software company.
